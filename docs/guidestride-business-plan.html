Executive Summary

GuideStride is a subscription-based business management platform built specifically for independent fishing guides. We give non-technical guides a simple, affordable way to manage clients, schedule trips, log catches, track payments, and grow their business — all from their phone.

The fishing guide industry is underserved by software. Most guides run their operations on spreadsheets, personal calendars, and pen and paper. The few software options that exist are expensive, bloated, and built for large lodges and outfitters — not the solo guide running 100 trips a year out of his truck.

Our position: GuideStride is the Netflix of guide management software — simple, familiar, and impossible to justify not having. At $20/month with no contracts and no percentage cuts, we are the most affordable purpose-built platform in the market.

The Problem

Independent fishing guides are running small businesses with no business infrastructure. They face three core problems every day:

1. Admin work eats their time

Scheduling, client communication, trip logging, and payment chasing — all done manually. A guide running 120 trips a year spends hundreds of hours on paperwork that should take minutes.

2. They can't prove their value

When a new client asks "are you a good guide?" there is no data to back it up. No catch history, no analytics, no track record. Just word of mouth and hope.

3. Existing software doesn't fit

The few platforms that exist are designed for large outfitters and lodges. They are expensive, confusing, and packed with features a solo guide will never use. Some take a percentage of every booking — a model that punishes guides for being successful.

The founding insight: Brett Adams spent countless hours trying to optimize guide business workflows using basic spreadsheets. The problem wasn't effort — it was that the right tool simply didn't exist at an accessible price point.

3

The Solution

GuideStride is built around one idea: make it feel like a phone app, not business software. Guides already use apps for everything. GuideStride fits into that mental model — fast, familiar, and designed for someone who has never used CRM software in their life.

Core Features at Launch

  • Client Management — contacts, trip history, outstanding balances
  • Trip Scheduling & Calendar — schedule, manage, and track all trips
  • Live Trip Mode — real-time fish logging, weather, photos, and notes from the water
  • Payment Tracking — cash, Venmo, Zelle, card, check — collect at trip end
  • Financial Analytics — revenue, outstanding, collection rate, YoY growth
  • Fishing Analytics — avg fish per trip, best day of week, top locations, species breakdown
  • Water Flow Data — real-time USGS river gauge data for trip planning
  • Outstanding Balance Tracker — client-level drill-down on who owes what

Planned Features (Roadmap)

  • Stripe billing integration (August 2026)
  • Expense tracking and tax-season reporting
  • Google Calendar and Apple Calendar sync
  • Guide website builder with monthly hosting
  • Affiliate and pro deal marketplace for fishing gear brands
  • Native mobile app via App Store (Capacitor)

  • Live Trip Mode — no competitor offers real-time on-water logging
  • Mobile-first — designed for a phone, not a desktop

Honest weakness: The Guide Network is $5 cheaper and has been in the market longer. We counter this with a superior product, personal onboarding, and a direct relationship with every customer.

Business Model

Primary Revenue — SaaS Subscription

  • $20/month per guide, billed monthly
  • 1-month free trial for all new signups (launching August 2026)
  • No contracts — cancel anytime

Pricing philosophy: GuideStride will never become expensive software. The brand promise is affordability. Positioned as a "Netflix subscription" — low enough to not think twice about, high enough to sustain and grow the business. We may lower to $15/month based on market feedback. We will not raise the price without adding significant new value.

Secondary Revenue Streams (Year 2+)

Website Hosting: Guides who want a professional website built and hosted by GuideStride pay a monthly hosting fee ($20–35/month). GuideStride builds the site. Guides keep it as long as they're subscribed.

Affiliate & Brand Partnerships: Partner with fishing gear manufacturers, fly shops, and outdoor brands. GuideStride negotiates pro deals and discounts for guides. We earn referral commissions. Guides get access to gear discounts. Brands get a direct channel to professional buyers.

In-App Advertising (Year 3+): Contextual, non-intrusive ads from fishing-relevant brands shown within the dashboard and analytics. Never interrupts the guide's workflow.

Long-Term Vision

In 5 years, GuideStride is the recognized platform for independent fishing guides across North America. The brand is known in guide circles the way Venmo is known for splitting bills — it's simply what you use.

The company runs with a team of 4–5, operates globally, and functions without requiring Brett and Kam in the day-to-day. Both founders spend 2–3 focused hours per day coaching the team, making product decisions, and pursuing strategic partnerships.

The product moat is the data. Once a guide has a year of trip history, catch analytics, and client records in GuideStride, switching costs become very high. The guide who signs up today gets a platform that saves them hours every week. The guide who has been on GuideStride for 3 years has 3 years of irreplaceable data — and would never dream of leaving. That is the business.
