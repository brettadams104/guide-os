## 1. Executive Summary

GuideStride is a subscription-based business management platform built specifically for independent fishing guides. We give non-technical guides a simple, affordable way to manage clients, schedule trips, log catches, track payments, and grow their business — all from their phone.

The fishing guide industry is underserved by software. Most guides run their operations on spreadsheets, personal calendars, and pen and paper. The few software options that exist are expensive, bloated, and built for large lodges and outfitters — not the solo guide running 100 trips a year out of his truck.

GuideStride fills that gap. At $20/month with no contracts, no percentage cuts, and no feature overload, we are the Netflix of guide management software — simple, familiar, and impossible to justify not having.

**Founders:** Brett Adams (Operations & Marketing) and Kam Grassi (Sales & Customer Relations)
**Headquarters:** United States (remote-first)
**Launch:** Beta — June 2026 | Paid launch — August 2026
**Business Model:** SaaS subscription, $20/month per guide
**Year 1 Goal:** 100 paying guides
**3-Year Goal:** 1,000 guides, nationally recognized brand

## 2. The Problem

Independent fishing guides are running small businesses with no business infrastructure. They face three core problems every day:

**1. Admin work eats their time.**
Scheduling, client communication, trip logging, payment chasing — all done manually. A guide who runs 120 trips a year spends hundreds of hours on paperwork that should take minutes.

**2. They can't prove their value.**
When a new client asks "are you a good guide?" there's no data to back it up. No track record, no catch history, no analytics. Just word of mouth and hope.

**3. Existing software doesn't fit.**
The few platforms that exist (The Guide Network, others) are designed for large outfitters and lodges. They're expensive, confusing, and packed with features a solo guide will never use. Some take a percentage of every booking — a model that punishes guides for being successful.

**The result:** Guides leave money on the table, lose clients they could have retained, and spend time on admin work instead of fishing.

---

## 3. The Solution

GuideStride is built around one idea: **make it feel like a phone app, not business software.**

Guides already use apps for everything — weather, maps, social media. GuideStride fits into that mental model. It's familiar, fast, and designed for someone who has never used CRM software in their life.

**Core features at launch:**
- Client management (contacts, trip history, outstanding balances)
- Trip scheduling and calendar
- Live Trip Mode — real-time fish logging, weather, photos, and notes from the water
- Payment tracking and collection (cash, Venmo, Zelle, card, check)
- Financial analytics (revenue, outstanding, collection rate, YoY growth)
- Fishing analytics (avg fish per trip, best day, top locations, species breakdown)
- Real-time USGS water flow data for river gauges
- Outstanding balance tracker with client-level drill-down

**Planned features (roadmap):**
- Stripe billing integration (August 2026)
- Expense tracking and revenue/expense reporting for tax season
- Personal calendar sync (Google Calendar / Apple Calendar)
- Guide website builder with monthly hosting fee
- Affiliate/pro deal marketplace for fishing gear brands
- Mobile app via Capacitor (App Store submission)

## 4. Market Opportunity

**Total Addressable Market (TAM):**
Approximately 50,000 licensed and active fishing guides operate across the United States. This includes river guides, lake guides, saltwater captains, and fly fishing operations of all sizes.

**Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM):**
Our focus is the independent and small-operation guide — estimated at 20,000 guides. These are full-time or serious part-time guides who run their own businesses without the resources or budget for enterprise software.

**Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) — Year 1:**
100 guides = 0.5% of SAM
This is a conservative and achievable target given our network, direct sales approach, and beta tester pipeline.

**Revenue at scale:**
- 100 guides × $20/month = $2,000 MRR / $24,000 ARR (Year 1)
- 500 guides × $20/month = $10,000 MRR / $120,000 ARR
- 1,000 guides × $20/month = $20,000 MRR / $240,000 ARR (Year 3)

Additional revenue streams at scale:
- Guide website hosting fees ($15–30/month per site)
- Affiliate commissions from fishing gear brands and pro deal partnerships
- Display advertising within the platform (non-intrusive, fishing-relevant)

## 6. Business Model

**Primary Revenue: SaaS Subscription**
- $20/month per guide
- 1-month free trial for all new signups (launching August 2026)
- No contracts, cancel anytime
- Pricing positioned as a "Netflix subscription" — low enough to not think twice about, high enough to sustain the business

**Pricing philosophy:**
GuideStride will never become expensive software. The brand promise is affordability. If market feedback supports it, we may lower to $15/month. We will not raise the price without adding significant new value.

**Secondary Revenue Streams (Year 2+):**

*Website hosting:*
Guides who want a professional website built and hosted by GuideStride pay a monthly hosting fee (target: $20–35/month). GuideStride builds the site, guides keep it as long as they're subscribed.

*Affiliate & brand partnerships:*
Partner with fishing gear manufacturers, fly shops, and outdoor brands. GuideStride negotiates pro deals and discounts for guides on the platform. We earn a referral commission on sales. Guides get access to gear discounts. Brands get a direct channel to professional buyers.

*In-app advertising (Year 3+):*
Contextual, non-intrusive advertising from fishing-adjacent brands shown within the analytics and dashboard. Opt-in only. Never interrupts the guide's workflow.

## 7. Go-To-Market Strategy

**Phase 1 — Beta (June–July 2026)**
- Deploy demo account and demo flow
- Onboard 5–10 guide friends as beta testers
- Collect feedback, fix issues, refine onboarding
- No charge during beta

**Phase 2 — Paid Launch (August 2026)**
- Activate Stripe billing and 1-month free trial
- Personal outreach to guide network
- Begin social media presence (Instagram, Facebook fishing groups, YouTube)
- Attend or sponsor local fishing expos and tournaments

**Phase 3 — Scale (September 2026 onward)**
- Referral program — guides who refer another guide get a free month
- Partnerships with fishing guide associations
- Content marketing — blog posts and YouTube tutorials targeting "how to run a fishing guide business" searches
- Paid social advertising once unit economics are validated

**Sales motion:**
Kam leads direct sales. Every new lead gets a personal demo call (the 15-minute GuideStride Demo Flow). After the call, they sign up for the free trial. After 2 weeks, a follow-up call checks in and addresses any questions. After the trial, they convert to paid or we learn why they didn't.

**Customer acquisition targets:**
- Month 1 (August): 5 paying guides
- Month 3: 20 paying guides
- Month 6: 50 paying guides
- Month 12: 100 paying guides

## 11. Risk Analysis

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Slow initial sales | Medium | High | Personal demo calls, beta tester referrals, guide network leverage |
| Guide churns after trial | Medium | Medium | Personal check-in call, discount offer, learn from feedback |
| Competitor copies features | Medium | Low | Speed of iteration, customer relationships, brand loyalty |
| Infrastructure costs spike | Low | Medium | Free tiers have significant headroom; upgrade only when revenue supports it |
| Founders run out of time | Low | High | 2–3 hour daily commitment is sustainable; scope is controlled |
| Guides resist technology | Medium | Medium | Personal onboarding calls, tutorial videos, extreme simplicity |

**Biggest acknowledged risk:**
Sales velocity — getting in front of enough guides efficiently. Mitigation: Kam owns this full-time, personal network is the starting pipeline, and the demo flow is designed to convert in 15 minutes.

## 12. Long-Term Vision

In 5 years, GuideStride is the recognized platform for independent fishing guides across North America. The brand is known in guide circles the way Venmo is known for splitting bills — it's just what you use.

The company runs with a team of 4–5, operates globally, and functions without requiring Brett and Kam in the day-to-day. Both founders spend 2–3 focused hours per day coaching the team, making product decisions, and pursuing strategic partnerships — not answering support emails.

Revenue comes from three sources: subscriptions, website hosting, and brand partnerships. The guide who signs up today gets a platform that saves them hours every week. The guide who's been on GuideStride for 3 years has 3 years of data, analytics, and client history that they can't get anywhere else — and would never dream of leaving.

**The product moat is the data.** Once a guide has a year of trip history, catch analytics, and client records in GuideStride, switching costs become very high. That's the business.
